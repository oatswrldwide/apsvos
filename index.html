What is an APS?

The Admission Point Score (APS) is calculated from your final NSC (National Senior Certificate) results. Your APS is the sum of your ratings for your six best subjects, excluding Life Orientation.

Rating Scale (NSC)

Percentage Rating
80 - 100% 7
70 - 79% 6
60 - 69% 5
50 - 59% 4
40 - 49% 3
30 - 39% 2
0 - 29% 1

Eduvos Admission Requirements

  • Minimum APS varies by qualification (typically 18-28)
  • NSC with Bachelor's Pass or Diploma Pass
  • Specific subject requirements may apply
  • English proficiency required

Frequently Asked Questions

What subjects count towards my APS?

Your six best subjects, excluding Life Orientation, count towards your APS. This means you can have up to 7 subjects, and we'll automatically calculate using the best 6.

What APS do I need for Eduvos?

Different qualifications require different APS scores. Bachelor's degrees typically require 23-28 APS, while diplomas may require 18-22 APS. Contact Eduvos for specific program requirements.

Can I apply with a lower APS?

Some programs may have flexible admission criteria. Contact Eduvos admissions to discuss your options and alternative pathways.

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator follows the official NSC rating scale. Your final APS will be calculated by Eduvos admissions based on your official results.
